1. "Mental illness and violence: Debunking myths, addressing realities"

- Type/Genre: Scholarly article

- Summary: This article addresses the common misconceptions linking mental illness with violence. It emphasizes that while individuals with serious mental illness may exhibit violent behavior, it is often intertwined with other factors such as substance use, adverse childhood experiences, and environmental factors. The article aims to inform health service psychologists and the public, debunking the myth that people with mental illness are largely responsible for mass shootings and other acts of mass violence.

- Credibility: The author, Tori DeAngelis, provides a comprehensive review supported by current research, making the arguments presented credible.

- Usefulness: The article is useful for understanding the complex relationship between mental illness and violence, providing evidence-based insights that could be crucial for a project exploring this topic.

2. "Gun Deaths, Violence and Mental Health"

- Type/Genre: Fact sheet/Report

- Summary: This text from Mental Health America discusses the relationship between firearm-related deaths and mental health conditions. It highlights that mental illness is not a significant predictor of violence towards others but is a predictor of suicide. The fact sheet counters the narrative that mental illness is a major factor in homicidal gun violence, stating that the vast majority of such violence is not carried out by individuals with mental illness.

- Credibility: Mental Health America is a reputable organization, and the fact sheet is based on scientific research and facts, lending credibility to the content.

- Usefulness: This text provides statistical evidence and scientific research that can be used to challenge misconceptions about mental illness and violence, making it a valuable source for anyone researching this area.
