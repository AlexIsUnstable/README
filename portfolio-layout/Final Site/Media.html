The Expression Of Violence through Media & Mental Health

Written by Alexander Humphrey

Have you ever thought about why action and horror movies are so popular? One reason is that the depiction of violence in media, particularly in movies, can have a cathartic effect on viewers and potentially offer benefits to mental health. This concept is rooted in the psychological phenomenon where exposure to fictional violence allows individuals to experience and process emotions in a safe environment. For example, horror movies can help people overcome real-world trauma by providing psychological responses similar to exposure therapy, a technique used to treat anxiety disorders.

Watching these films can lead to improved fear responses and provide relief from anxiety and stress, as viewers learn about their own fear responses and how to regulate their emotions (Johnson, Nicole. "How Horror Movies Can Help People Overcome Real-World Trauma." National Geographic, 30 Oct. 2020).

Additionally, engagement with violent themes in movies can serve as a form of emotional release, helping viewers to confront and manage aggressive impulses in a non-destructive manner. The aforementioned aggression catharsis hypothesis suggests that watching violent media may allow people to "vent" their aggressive inclinations, leading to less aggressive behavior afterward. The expression of violent themes in movies can be both cathartic and beneficial to mental health, offering viewers a therapeutic avenue to explore and resolve emotional conflicts in a manner that promotes healing and well-being.

Contrary to popular belief, studies indicate that there is no significant link between playing violent video games and increased aggression or mental health issues like anxiety, depression, or ADHD.

In fact, engaging in virtual combat or aggressive scenarios within a game can provide players with a safe space to process and release emotions, potentially leading to a reduction in real-world aggression and an improvement in mood (Ramo, Danielle Ph.D. "Is Playing Violent Video Games Related to Teens' Mental Health?" Psychology Today, 25 Feb. 2021).

Additionally, the interactive nature of video games allows players to experience control over their actions, which can be empowering and stress-relieving. This sense of control, combined with the immersive storytelling found in many games, can lead to a deeper emotional engagement and a more profound cathartic experience. The emotional release obtained from playing violent video games such as DOOM, where the player literally rips and tears demons apart, can serve as a coping mechanism, helping individuals to manage their feelings and reduce stress ("Violent Video Games and Young People" Harvard Health Publishing, Oct. 2010).

Therefore, while the debate continues, there is growing evidence to suggest that violent themes in video games can serve a cathartic purpose and have positive effects on mental health.
